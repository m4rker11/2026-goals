# Chapter 14: Compound Verbs I: Benefaction

**Using secondary verbs to add shade.**

---

## 14A. Lena (Take) - Self Focus

### 14A-i. Absorption

The action is internalized or absorbed by the subject.

> **Example:**
> "Samajh lo" (Understand it *for yourself*)

---

### 14A-ii. Internal Action

The action benefits the doer directly.

> **Example:**
> "Kha lo" (Eat up)

**Key Difference:** "Khao" is just a command to eat; "Kha lo" is an inviting suggestion with warmth.

---

### 14A-iii. Reflexive Benefit

The subject keeps or retains something for themselves.

> **Example:**
> "Rakh lo" (Keep it *for yourself*)

---

## 14B. Dena (Give) - Other Focus

### 14B-i. Direction Outward

The action moves away from the subject.

> **Example:**
> "Phenk do" (Throw it away)

---

### 14B-ii. Assistance

The action is performed to help or serve someone else.

> **Example:**
> "Padh do" (Read it out *for me*)

**Key Difference:**
- "Padh lo" = Read it yourself
- "Padh do" = Read it to/for someone else

---

### 14B-iii. Finality of Exit

Expresses letting go or releasing.

> **Example:**
> "Jane do" (Let it go)

---

## 14C. Modal Nuance of Vectors

### 14C-i. Command Softener

Compound verbs make imperatives sound less harsh and more polite.

> **Example:**
> "De do na" (Please give it)

**Key Difference:** Compound verbs soften commands and make them more socially acceptable.

---

### 14C-ii. Dena vs Lena Conflict

The choice between these vectors changes the beneficiary.

> **Example:**
> - "Likh lo" (Write it down *for your own notes*)
> - "Likh do" (Write it *for me*)

---

### 14C-iii. Tense Restrictions

Compound verbs have limitations in negative sentences.

> **Example:**
> - "Main nahin gaya" (Correct)
> - "Main nahin ja gaya" (Incorrect)

**Key Difference:** You generally don't use these compound vectors in negative sentences.
