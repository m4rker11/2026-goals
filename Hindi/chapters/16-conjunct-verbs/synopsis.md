# Unit 16: Conjunct Verbs

## Key Concepts

### Conjunct Verbs with karna/hona
Two types of conjunct verb formation:

**Type 1: Adjective/Noun + karna/hona**
- Transitive: thik karna (to fix), saf karna (to clean), shuru karna (to start)
- Intransitive: shuru hona (to begin), thik hona (to be fixed)
- Example: Chotu ko apni saikil thik karni hai (Chotu has to fix his bike)

**Type 2: Object + ka/ki/ke + Base Noun + karna**
- Base noun is "possessed" by object: Ram bacchon ki dekhbhal karta hai (Ram looks after the children)
- Gender of base noun determines postposition: mai Ram ki madad karunga

**Agreement Rules:**
- In ne-constructions, verb agrees with base noun: Ram ne bacchon ki dekhbhal ki
- In passive, verb agrees with base noun: naye ghar ki talash ki jaegi

### Common Conjunct Verbs
- intazar karna (to wait for) - masculine base
- istemal karna (to use) - masculine base
- picha karna (to follow/chase) - masculine base
- talash karna (to search) - feminine base
- dekhbhal karna (to look after) - feminine base
- ninda karna (to blame) - feminine base
- koshish karna (to try) - feminine base, requires oblique infinitive

### koshish karna Pattern
- Preceding infinitive must be oblique: fon karne ki koshish ki (tried to telephone)
- Example: samay par ane ki koshish karna (Try to come on time)

### kahin "Somewhere" - Multiple Uses
- Basic: kahin aur (somewhere else), kahin nahi (nowhere), kahin bhi (anywhere)
- "Somehow": kahin paise mil jae to... (If somehow we get the money...)
- "Considerably": dusra makan kahin zyada mahanga tha (much dearer)
- "Lest": kahin...na + subjunctive: mai dar raha tha ki kahin meri patni na aa jae
- Rhetorical impossibility: kahin koi insan apne bacche ko marega?
- Sharpens insults: badmash kahin ka! (Wretched scoundrel!)

### Word Repetition
- **Distribution**: ek ek rupya do (one rupee each), alag alag baitho (sit separately)
- **Variety**: tarah tarah ke janvar (various kinds of animals), kaun kaun mila? (which various people?)
- **Emphasis**: saf saf batao (tell plainly), bahut bahut dhanyavad, garam garam chai (piping hot tea)

### Echo Words
- Standard v- echoes: chai-vai (tea etc.), pensil-vensil (pencil or something)
- Generalizes meaning: meeting-sheeting (meeting or anything of the kind)
- Established pairs: naukar-chakar (servants), milna-julna (to associate), jan-bujhkar (deliberately)

### Correlative Conjunctions
- **ya to...ya** = either...or: ya to pi lo ya mujhe pine do
- **na...na** = neither...nor: Chotu na alsi hai na bevquf
- **chahe...chahe/ya** = whether...or: khana chahe achha ho chahe kharab
- **chahe...kyon na ho** = no matter what: chahe jo bhi ho, tumhe nadi ko par karna hoga
