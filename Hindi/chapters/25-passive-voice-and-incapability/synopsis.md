# Chapter 25: Passive Voice & Incapability

**Goal:** Shifting the focus.

---

## 25A. Standard Passive (Jaana)

### 25A-i. Formation

**Structure:** Main Verb (Perfective) + *Jaana*

> **Example:** "Chor pakda gaya"
> *(The thief was caught.)*

---

### 25A-ii. Habitual Passive

> **Example:** "Diwali manayi jati hai"
> *(Diwali is celebrated.)*

**Key Difference:** Use *jati hai* for ongoing/habitual passive actions.

---

### 25A-iii. Agent "Dwara" (Formal)

> **Example:** "Police ke dwara"
> *(By the police)*

**Full sentence:** "Chor police ke dwara pakda gaya."
*(The thief was caught by the police.)*

---

## 25B. Incapability Passive (Mujhse...)

### 25B-i. Negative Capacity

> **Example:** "Mujhse utha nahin jata"
> *(I cannot get up.)*

**Key Difference:** Implies "It is not done by me" due to weakness, illness, or inability - not choice.

**Structure:** Subject (with *se*) + Verb (passive) + *nahin jata*

---

### 25B-ii. Sense Verbs

> **Example:** "Dekha nahin jata"
> *(Cannot bear to watch.)*

Common expressions:

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| Dekha nahin jata | Cannot bear to watch |
| Suna nahin jata | Cannot bear to hear |
| Saha nahin jata | Cannot bear/tolerate |

---

## 25C. Impersonal Passive

### 25C-i. Suggestion

> **Example:** "Chalo, dekha jaye"
> *(Come, let it be seen / let's see.)*

---

### 25C-ii. Permission

> **Example:** "Shuru kiya jaye?"
> *(Shall it be started?)*

**Key Difference:** This is a polite, formal way to ask permission or make suggestions.

---

### 25C-iii. General Statement

> **Example:** "Kaha jata hai ki..."
> *(It is said that...)*

Other common impersonal passives:

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| Mana jata hai | It is believed |
| Dekha jata hai | It is seen |
| Samjha jata hai | It is understood |

---

## Summary: Passive Voice Types

| Type | Structure | Usage |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Standard Passive | Verb + *jaana* | Focus on action, not agent |
| Habitual Passive | Verb + *jata/jati hai* | Regular/ongoing passives |
| Incapability | *Mujhse* + Verb + *nahin jata* | Physical/emotional inability |
| Impersonal | Verb + *jaye/jata hai* | General statements, suggestions |
