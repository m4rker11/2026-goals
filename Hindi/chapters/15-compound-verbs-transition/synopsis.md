# Chapter 15: Compound Verbs II: Transition & Force

**Suddenness, Violence, and Mistakes.**

---

## 15A. Jana (Go) - Completion

### 15A-i. Change of State

*Jana* marks a transition from one state to another.

> **Example:**
> "Vo so gaya" (He fell asleep)

**Key Difference:**
- "Soya" = Slept (simple fact)
- "So gaya" = Transition *into* sleep (emphasizes the change)

---

### 15A-ii. Totality

Expresses that an action was done completely or exhaustively.

> **Example:**
> "Vo sab kuch kha gaya" (He ate *everything* up)

---

### 15A-iii. Loss of Control

Indicates that something happened beyond the subject's control.

> **Example:**
> "Main bhool gaya" (I forgot)

The *gaya* suggests the forgetting happened involuntarily.

---

## 15B. Padna (Fall) - Suddenness

### 15B-i. Accidental Action

*Padna* adds a sense of suddenness or accident.

> **Example:**
> "Vo gir pada" (He fell down *suddenly*)

---

### 15B-ii. Emotional Outburst

Expresses sudden, uncontrollable emotional reactions.

> **Example:**
> "Vo ro padi" (She burst into tears)

**Key Difference:**
- "Royi" = She cried (simple)
- "Ro padi" = Sudden, uncontrollable crying

---

### 15B-iii. Encounter

Used in expressions about becoming visible or apparent.

> **Example:**
> "Dikayi padna" (To become visible / to appear)

---

## 15C. Dalna, Uthna, Baithna

### 15C-i. Dalna (Force)

Adds intensity, violence, or finality to an action.

> **Example:**
> "Maar dala" (Killed *violently/completely*)

---

### 15C-ii. Uthna (Eruption)

Expresses sudden arising or flaring up.

> **Example:**
> "Dard utha" (Pain flared up)

---

### 15C-iii. Baithna (Regret)

Indicates an action done hastily that the subject regrets.

> **Example:**
> "Main use kho baitha" (I lost him - *and regret it*)

The *baithna* compound implies carelessness and subsequent regret.
