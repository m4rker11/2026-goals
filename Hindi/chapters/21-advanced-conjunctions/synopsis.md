# Chapter 21: Advanced Conjunctions

**Why this matters:** Once you know "If/Then" (Conditionals), you need "Although," "Because," and "Otherwise" to debate.

---

## 21A. Contrast & Concession

### 21A-i. "Halanki... Phir bhi" (Although... Still)

> **Example:** "Halanki vo bimar tha, phir bhi vo aaya."
> *(Although he was sick, still he came.)*

**Key Difference:** A formal structure for nuanced disagreement.

---

### 21A-ii. "Magar/Par/Lekin" Hierarchy

| Word | Usage |
|------|-------|
| *Lekin* | But - standard |
| *Par* | But - quick/casual |
| *Magar* | But - heavier/literary |

---

### 21A-iii. "Balki" (Rather/In fact)

> **Example:** "Vo gussa nahin, balki dukhi hai."
> *(He isn't angry; rather/in fact, he is sad.)*

**Key Difference:** Used to correct a previous assumption.

---

## 21B. Reason & Result

### 21B-i. "Kyunki" vs "Chunki"

| Word | Usage |
|------|-------|
| *Kyunki* | Because - mid-sentence |
| *Chunki* | Since/Given that - starts sentence |

> **Example:** "Chunki main bimar tha, isliye nahin aaya."
> *(Since I was sick, therefore I didn't come.)*

---

### 21B-ii. "Isliye" (Therefore)

> **Example:** "Main busy hoon, isliye nahin aa sakta."
> *(I am busy, therefore I cannot come.)*

---

### 21B-iii. "Natijan" (As a result)

**Key Difference:** Formal business/news Hindi.

---

## 21C. Warning & Conditions

### 21C-i. "Varna" (Otherwise)

> **Example:** "Padhai karo, varna fail ho jaoge."
> *(Study, or else you'll fail.)*

---

### 21C-ii. "Nahin to" (If not then...)

**Key Difference:** Colloquial version of *Varna*.

---

### 21C-iii. "Chahe... Ya..." (Whether... Or...)

> **Example:** "Chahe tum aao ya na aao, main jaonga."
> *(Whether you come or not, I will go.)*
