# Chapter 9: Past Tense Deep Dive

*Moving beyond "I went".*

---

## 9A. Simple Past

### 9A-i. Intransitive (Subject Focus)

> **Example:** "Main gaya."

---

### 9A-ii. Transitive (Object Focus + Ne)

> **Example:** "Maine khaya."

**Key difference:** The fundamental split in Hindi Past tense.

---

### 9A-iii. The "Tha" Auxiliary Addition

> **Example:** "Main gaya tha" (I had gone / I went).

**Key difference:** Adding *tha* pushes it further back or emphasizes completion (Past Perfect).

---

## 9B. Past Continuous

### 9B-i. Background Action

> **Example:** "Jab main aaya, vo so raha tha."

---

### 9B-ii. Interrupted Action

> **Example:** "Main ja raha tha, lekin ruk gaya."

---

### 9B-iii. Habitual Continuous

> **Example:** "Vo aksar gunguna raha hota tha." (He was often humming).

**Key difference:** Rare/Advanced structure combining habit + continuous.

---

## 9C. Past Habitual (Used to)

### 9C-i. "Ta tha" Construction

> **Example:** "Main cricket khelta tha" (I used to play).

---

### 9C-ii. Repeated Instance

> **Example:** "Hum roz milte the."

---

### 9C-iii. Discontinued State

> **Example:** "Main pehle mota hota tha" (I used to be fat).

**Key difference:** Using *Hota tha* for past states that are no longer true.
