## Topics Covered

### 1. Postposition Rules (Review & Reinforcement)

| Rule | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Masculine -a + postposition | -a → -e | larka → larke ko |
| Any plural + postposition | adds -on | ladke → ladkon, ladkiyan → ladkiyon |
| Feminine singular + postposition | no change | ladki → ladki ko |

**Key practice:**
- "mere bhai ke kamre mein" (in my brother's room) - cascading changes
- Both "mera" and "ka" change because each has a postposition following

---

### 2. "Ki wajah se" (Because of) - Full Paradigm

| Pronoun | Form | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| this | iski wajah se | iski wajah se nahi aaya |
| that | uski wajah se | uski wajah se late hua |
| these | inki wajah se | |
| those | unki wajah se | |
| that girl | us ladki ki wajah se | |
| that boy | us ladke ki wajah se | (ladka → ladke) |
| those girls | un ladkiyon ki wajah se | |
| those boys | un ladkon ki wajah se | |

**Note:** "ki" stays constant because "wajah" is feminine. The preceding noun/pronoun takes oblique.

**Alternative:** "isliye" = therefore/that's why (more common in speech)

### 3. Word Order in Complex Sentences

**Standard order:**
```
Subject → People (ke saath/ke binaa) → Time* → Place → Nouns+Postpositions → Adjectives → Negation → Verb
```
*Time can optionally go first

**Example breakdown:**
"She is here with her family for an event"
- ❌ "Vo idhar event ke liye hai apne parivaar ke saath"
- ✅ "Vo apne parivaar ke saath yahaan event ke liye hai"

**Key insight:** Talk about people first, then place, then purpose.

---

### 4. Negation Placement

**Critical rule:** नहीं goes directly BEFORE the verb, not before the noun in compound verbs.

| Wrong | Correct | Translation |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| nahi kaam kar raha hoon | kaam nahi kar raha hoon | I am not working |
| nahi saaf kar rahi thi | saaf nahi kar rahi thi | She was not cleaning |

**Why:** In "kaam karna", "kaam" is the object and "kar" is the actual verb. Negation precedes the verb.

### 5. Tu/Tum/Aap - Grammar & Agreement

| Register | Pronoun | Verb | Possessive | Usage |
|----------|---------|------|------------|-------|
| Informal | Tu | hai | mera/meri | God, very close friends, insult |
| Casual | Tum | ho | mere/meri | Same age, friends, cousins, girlfriend |
| Formal | Aap | hain | mere/meri | Strangers, elders, respect |

**Critical insight:** Tum and Aap are **grammatically plural** even when referring to one person.

| Wrong | Correct | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| tum mera dost ho | tum mere dost ho | tum is grammatically plural |
| aap mera dost hain | aap mere dost hain | aap is grammatically plural |
| tu mere dost hai | tu mera dost hai | tu is singular (only one that takes mera) |

**Book reference:** Table 1.1 in the textbook for full paradigm.

### 6. Vala/Vali - Multiple Uses

#### Use 1: Profession/Type (already known)
- gadi vala = driver
- dukaan vala = shopkeeper
- cab vala = cab driver
- dudh vala = milkman

#### Use 2: Specific Reference (NEW)
When referring back to something mentioned earlier:
- "vo vala phone" = that specific phone (we discussed)
- "vo vali meri dost" = that friend of mine (I mentioned before)
- "vo vala backpack upar hai" = that backpack (you know which one) is upstairs

#### Use 3: With Adjectives (NEW)
- "tumhara kala vala sweater kahan hai?" = where is your black sweater (the black one)?
- Points to a specific item with that quality

**Gender agreement:** vala (m), vali (f), vale (m pl/oblique), valiyan (f pl)

**vs. ye/vo:**
- ye/vo = this/that (present, pointing)
- vala = that specific one (referencing something from context/conversation)

### 7. Sentence Translation Strategies

**Problem:** Long English sentences with multiple clauses.

**Solution discussed:** Break into chunks, but follow Hindi word order.

**Example:** "I had a class with one of my students from America"

Multiple valid constructions:
1. "Meri ek class thi American student ke saath"
2. "America se ek student ke saath class thi"
3. "Meri American student ke saath class thi"

**Key:** Simplify "one of my students from America" → "American student" or "America se student"
