# Chapter 22: Participles (Connecting Actions)

**Goal:** Fluid sentences without "And".

---

## 22A. Conjunctive Participle (Kar)

### 22A-i. Sequence

> **Example:** "Nahakar aao"
> *(Bathe and come / Come after bathing.)*

---

### 22A-ii. Manner

> **Example:** "Sochkar bolo"
> *(Speak thoughtfully / after thinking.)*

---

### 22A-iii. Stem Only (Colloquial)

> **Example:** "Dekh ke" instead of "Dekh kar"

**Key Difference:** In casual speech, *ke* replaces *kar*.

---

## 22B. Imperfective Adjective (Ta hua)

### 22B-i. Active State

> **Example:** "Daudta hua ghoda"
> *(Running horse)*

---

### 22B-ii. While doing (Adverbial)

> **Example:** "Vo rote hue bola"
> *(He spoke while crying.)*

---

### 22B-iii. Agreement

> **Example:** "Roti hui ladki"
> *(Crying girl)*

**Key Difference:** The participle must agree in gender with the noun:
- Masculine: *rota hua*
- Feminine: *roti hui*

---

## 22C. Perfective Adjective (Ya hua)

### 22C-i. Finished State

> **Example:** "Gira hua phool"
> *(Fallen flower)*

---

### 22C-ii. Static Posture

> **Example:** "Baitha hua"
> *(Seated)*

---

### 22C-iii. Object Description

> **Example:** "Likha hua kagaz"
> *(Written paper)*

**Key Difference:** This describes a completed action's result, not an ongoing action.
